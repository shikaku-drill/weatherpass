Rainy days, heatwaves & booking — quick answers

What can you do in Tokyo when it rains?

Plenty — Tokyo is one of the best rainy-day cities in the world. Fully indoor picks like teamLab Planets, Sumida Aquarium, the Sumida Hokusai Museum, sushi-making classes and covered shopping streets all run rain or shine. WEATHERPASS reads the live forecast, shows only what's still open and reachable today, and compares Klook, KKday and Viator so you book the lowest price.

Klook vs KKday vs Viator — which is cheapest for Japan attractions?

It changes per venue and per day: each site runs its own promotions, and the cheapest one for teamLab is often not the cheapest for Tokyo Skytree. That's why we show all three prices side-by-side for every experience and highlight today's lowest — typically saving 10–35% versus booking on the first site you open.

What should you do in Japan during a heatwave?

When it's 33–38°C, treat the heat like rain: go fully indoor and air-conditioned. Digital-art museums, aquariums, indoor food classes and spa complexes are the classic escapes. WEATHERPASS switches to heatwave mode automatically above 30°C and puts cool indoor experiences first, sorted by how fast you can get out of the sun.
